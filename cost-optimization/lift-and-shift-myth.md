# Why Lift & Shift is the Most Misunderstood Cloud Strategy

Most enterprises think “Lift & Shift” is the safest way to move to cloud.

It isn’t.

While working through a cloud transformation scenario (5000+ VMs, mixed Windows & Linux), one thing became very clear:

👉 Moving workloads *as-is* to cloud often increases cost instead of reducing it.

## Here’s why:

- On-prem systems are over-provisioned for peak usage  
- In cloud, you pay for what you run—not what you bought  
- Always-on environments (especially Dev/Test) silently inflate costs  
- Storage and licensing decisions behave very differently in cloud  

## So what should “Lift & Shift” actually look like?

Not a blind migration.

A **lightly optimized rehost strategy**:

- Right-size compute based on real usage  
- Shut down non-prod workloads when not in use  
- Use licensing benefits where applicable  
- Rethink availability using cloud-native constructs  

## The goal is simple:

- Move fast  
- Reduce risk  
- Control cost from Day 1  

Not everything needs modernization on Day 1.

But nothing should be moved without thinking.
